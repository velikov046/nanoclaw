## NOTES

- "Tu m'étonnes" is rendered as "tell me about it" (colloquial agreement) rather than the literal "you surprise me," which would invert the meaning.
- "Ma foule" is an informal, affectionate way of addressing one's audience; "my crowd" preserves that warmth without sounding stiff.
- "J'ai trop hâte" uses "trop" as an intensifier in informal speech, not as a negative; "I can't wait" captures the register accurately.
