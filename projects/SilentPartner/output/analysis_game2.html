SUMMARY

The fascist team — Rachel (Hitler), Sullivan (Fascist), Charlotte (almost certainly the third Fascist) — played a textbook fascist game structured around misdirection and paced liberal sacrifice. Rachel's opening play was high-risk and high-yield: three fascist policies in round one is an enormous board advantage, but it concentrated suspicion on the Rachel–Tom government immediately. The fascists weathered that scrutiny by having Sullivan and Charlotte pass liberal policies in their early governments, deliberately burning fascist cards to buy credibility. Charlotte's liberal-passing government with Sullivan (~2450s) is the clearest example — a confirmed fascist president discarding her own fascist policy to make her co-fascist chancellor look clean.

The game's decisive mistake was Charlotte's final government with John. Charlotte had a genuine opportunity to play liberal — she could have passed John one liberal and one fascist — but chose (or was forced by deck luck) to pass him two fascists, and then lied about it. The lie is what collapses the alibi. A liberal who makes a bad draw apologises; Charlotte repeated the false claim twice before John's flat denial exposed it. Had Charlotte told the truth ("I drew two fascists and a liberal, discarded the liberal — I know it's bad"), she might have survived the subsequent execution deliberation. The lie handed Holly the confirmatory evidence she needed to choose Rachel over Sullivan as the Hitler-kill target.

The dominant hypothesis holds cleanly: Rachel–Sullivan–Charlotte is the fascist team, with Tom, Dom, Holly, and John as liberals. The biggest remaining uncertainty is whether Charlotte's final government was intentional fascist play or catastrophic liberal misplay followed by a panicked lie. The lie itself is the only piece of evidence that can't be explained by bad luck alone — it is the single data point that most separates "Charlotte is fascist" from "Charlotte is an unlucky liberal." If Charlotte had been honest about her draw, this analysis would be genuinely undecided between Charlotte and Tom as the third evil player. She wasn't, so it isn't.
