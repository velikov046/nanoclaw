GLOSSARY

Term Meaning here
deciding frame The final frame of a match when both players are level; whoever wins it wins the match.
sessions In major snooker matches, play is split into sessions of roughly 8 frames each, with breaks between them.
cannon A shot where the cue ball contacts two object balls without potting either, used to nudge balls into better positions tactically.
long pot Potting a ball from a long distance across the table; high-skill, higher-risk than a short pot.
the black The black ball, worth 7 points, the highest-value ball on the table. Attacking it in a deciding frame is a calculated gamble.
the white The cue ball -- the ball the player strikes with their cue. Losing control of it means losing position for the next shot.
snooker yourself Accidentally leaving yourself unable to see the ball you need to hit next, caused by your own shot rather than the opponent's.
