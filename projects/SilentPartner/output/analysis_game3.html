SUMMARY

The dominant hypothesis — Dom as Hitler, John as regular Fascist, the remaining four Liberal — is robust by the end of the transcript. What makes this game analytically interesting is not the identification of the fascist team (Dom's role was dealt on camera and John's silence was flagged by two players independently early on) but the mechanism of the Liberal win. No one was investigated or executed. The liberals won entirely through card accounting and deliberate testing: Holly and Charlotte confirmed each other through two separate governments, Rachel converted her own Fascist reputation into a trust signal by visibly sacrificing a tile, and Sullivan connected that confirmed block to Dom by documenting a behavioral tell rather than relying on deduction. The fascist team had almost no viable moves — the deck was heavily liberal at the top, every government that could have forced a fascist policy was between players who would immediately cross-check their hands, and Dom's only realistic path to the chancellorship narrowed each round.

The Fascist partnership's strategic failure is clearest in Dom's post-game debrief (~4185–4230s). He describes watching liberals confirm each other, running out of plausible chancellor options, and banking on an opponent having three fascists — essentially hoping for deck composition to save him rather than executing a plan. This is the picture of a Hitler who lost his partner's cover early: John's silence may have kept him hidden, but it also meant Dom was operating without visible fascist support in the critical middle rounds, unable to build a credible path to his own chancellorship.

The single biggest uncertainty is John's definitive confirmation. Two players independently flagged him, the silent-speaker tell is strong, and the one fascist policy enacted fits his suspected presidential slot — but the post-game reveal in the transcript does not explicitly name John as the Fascist. If John turns out to be Liberal, Dom's debrief would imply he had no viable partner at all, which reshapes the entire read of why the fascists played so passively. That confirmation, or its absence, is the one piece of information that fully closes the analysis.
