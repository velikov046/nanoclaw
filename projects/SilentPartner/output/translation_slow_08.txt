## NOTES

- "Tu m'étonnes" is rendered as "tell me about it" rather than its literal "you surprise me," which would be misleading; the idiomatic use here is emphatic agreement.
- "Ma foule" ("my crowd") is a casual, affectionate address to the audience; register kept informal to match.
- "Faire la grasse matinée" has no single English equivalent; "sleep in" captures the meaning concisely without padding.
