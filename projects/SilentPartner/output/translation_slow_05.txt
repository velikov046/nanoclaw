## NOTES

- "Tu m'étonnes" is rendered as "I'm not surprised" or "tell me about it" depending on context; both readings were active in this session.
- "Ma foule" (literally "my crowd") is an informal address to the audience, kept close to the source rather than normalized to "everyone" or "guys."
- "Faire la grasse matinée" has no single English equivalent; "sleep in" was used consistently throughout.
