## NOTES

- "Tu m'étonnes" is rendered as "tell me about it" rather than the literal "you amaze me" -- the idiomatic force is agreement and commiseration, not surprise.
- "ma foule" as a direct address to the audience ("my crowd") preserves the informal warmth of the original; "my audience" would be technically accurate but flatter in register.
- "j'ai trop hâte" uses colloquial intensifier "trop" -- "I can't wait" captures the enthusiasm better than the literal "I'm in too much of a hurry."
